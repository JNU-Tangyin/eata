\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

This section presents a comprehensive evaluation of EATA. We design our experiments to answer the three research questions (RQs) proposed in Section~\ref{sec:intro}:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{RQ1 (Interpretability)}: Can EATA discover meaningful mathematical expressions that domain experts can understand?
    \item \textbf{RQ2 (Guidance)}: Does the neural-guided MCTS effectively navigate the vast search space compared to evolutionary or random methods?
    \item \textbf{RQ3 (Performance)}: Can interpretable models compete with black-box deep learning in terms of trading profitability?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}

\subsubsection{Datasets}
We utilize two distinct datasets to evaluate performance and generalization:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Core Dataset (31 Stocks)}: High-volume stocks from diverse sectors (Tech, Finance, Healthcare) covering Jan 2020--Dec 2024. This includes the COVID-19 crash (Bear), 2021 recovery (Bull), and 2022 correction (Volatile).
    \item \textbf{Generalization Dataset (S\&P 100)}: The full S\&P 100 constituents to test robustness across a broader market universe.
\end{enumerate}
Data is split 70\%/30\% for training/testing.

\subsubsection{Baselines}
We compare against 8 baselines across four categories. Deep learning models (LSTM, Transformer, PPO) are tuned via grid search: Learning Rate $\{10^{-3}, 10^{-4}, 10^{-5}\}$, Hidden Dim $\{64, 128, 256\}$.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Traditional}: Buy \& Hold, MACD.
    \item \textbf{Time Series}: ARIMA.
    \item \textbf{Machine Learning}: LightGBM, Genetic Programming (GP).
    \item \textbf{Deep Learning}: LSTM, Transformer, PPO (RL).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Case Studies: Discovered Alpha}
We examine specific expressions discovered by EATA to confirm they align with financial intuition.

\textbf{Case 1 (Trend Following):}
\begin{equation}
e_{trend} = \text{mom}(x_{close}, 10) \times \mathbb{I}(\text{rsi}(x_{close}, 14) < 70)
\end{equation}
\textit{Interpretation:} Capture momentum, but only when the asset is not overbought.

\textbf{Case 2 (Mean Reversion):}
\begin{equation}
e_{rev} = \frac{x_{close} - \text{ma}(x_{close}, 20)}{\text{vol}(x_{close}, 20)}
\end{equation}
\textit{Interpretation:} A classic Bollinger Band-style reversion signal, scaled by volatility.

\subsection{Mechanism Analysis: Why EATA Works? (RQ2)}

\subsubsection{Effect of Wasserstein Profit Head}
Standard symbolic regression optimizes MSE. We argue this is insufficient for trading. Figure~\ref{fig:dist} compares the return distribution predicted by EATA vs. an MSE-optimized variant. EATA (Blue) successfully captures the heavy tails (kurtosis) of the actual returns (Gray), whereas the MSE model (Red) assumes a Gaussian distribution, underestimating risk.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\columnwidth]{figures/fig3_return_distribution.pdf}
\caption{Return Distribution Modeling. EATA captures the ``fat tails'' of real market returns (Gray Area), unlike MSE-based models (Red Dashed) which assume normality. \fake{KDE plot of test set residuals.}}
\label{fig:dist}
\end{figure}

Table~\ref{tab:ablation_loss} quantifies this benefit. Removing the Profit Head drops the Sharpe Ratio from 0.89 to 0.62.

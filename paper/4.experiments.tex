\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

This section presents a comprehensive evaluation of EATA. We design our experiments to answer the three research questions (RQs) proposed in Section~\ref{sec:intro}:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{RQ1 (Objective)}: Does Wasserstein-based profit optimization outperform standard MSE-based accuracy optimization?
    \item \textbf{RQ2 (Guidance)}: Does the neural-guided MCTS effectively navigate the search space to find profitable strategies?
    \item \textbf{RQ3 (Performance)}: Can interpretable symbolic models achieve competitive risk-adjusted returns compared to black-box deep learning?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}

\subsubsection{Datasets}
We utilize a core dataset of \textbf{500 high-volume stocks} from the S\&P 500, covering Technology, Finance, and Healthcare sectors. The evaluation period spans from \textbf{Jan 1, 2020 to Dec 31, 2024}, capturing diverse market regimes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{2020}: COVID-19 crash and rapid recovery (High Volatility).
    \item \textbf{2021}: Strong Bull market.
    \item \textbf{2022}: Fed tightening and market correction (Bear).
    \item \textbf{2023-2024}: AI-driven rally and stabilization.
\end{itemize}
Daily OHLCV data is split temporally: the first 70\% for training (sliding window) and the subsequent 30\% for out-of-sample testing.

\subsubsection{Baselines}
We compare EATA against 8 baselines across four categories:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Traditional}: Buy \& Hold, MACD (tuned).
    \item \textbf{Statistical}: ARIMA (5,1,0).
    \item \textbf{Machine Learning}: LightGBM, Genetic Programming (GP, via Operon).
    \item \textbf{Deep Learning}: LSTM, Transformer (tuned via grid search).
    \item \textbf{Reinforcement Learning}: PPO (FinRL implementation).
    \item \textbf{Symbolic Regression}: NEMoTS (Neural Equation MCTS, our base framework).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Finding.} Removing the Profit Head causes a sharp drop in Sharpe Ratio (0.83 $\rightarrow$ 0.66) and a significant increase in Maximum Drawdown (36.5\% $\rightarrow$ 45.4\%). This confirms that the Profit Head acts as a crucial regularizer, steering the search away from strategies that might have high MSE accuracy but dangerous drawdown characteristics.

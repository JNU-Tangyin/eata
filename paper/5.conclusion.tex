\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented EATA, a neural-guided symbolic regression framework that explicitly optimizes for trading profitability via Wasserstein distance. Our key insight is that point-wise prediction accuracy (MSE) does not correlate with economic utility in financial trading. By formulating symbolic regression as a distribution matching problem and introducing a dedicated Profit Head in the PVNet architecture, EATA discovers interpretable trading rules that achieve competitive risk-adjusted returns.

\textbf{Summary of Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item We formalized trading as a \textbf{distribution matching problem} using Wasserstein distance, providing theoretical justification for why this metric captures tail risk and directional asymmetry better than MSE or KL divergence.
    \item We extended NEMoTS with a \textbf{three-headed PVNet} (Policy-Value-Profit), demonstrating that explicit profit optimization improves Sharpe ratio by 0.25 (from 0.58 to 0.83) over accuracy-only optimization on real market data.
    \item We conducted \textbf{extensive empirical validation} on 31 high-volume stocks over 4 years, with in-depth ablations validating the necessity of the Profit Head and domain-specific grammar.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Limitations.} Several limitations remain:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Computational Cost}: MCTS requires significant compute time per stock compared to gradient-based methods, limiting applicability to high-frequency trading.
    \item \textbf{Grammar Design}: Fixed-window operators require domain expertise to select. Automated grammar evolution remains future work.
    \item \textbf{Single-Asset Focus}: Current implementation treats stocks independently. Portfolio-level optimization and cross-asset relationships are not modeled.
    \item \textbf{Extreme Regimes}: While EATA handles moderate non-stationarity via sliding windows, extreme exogenous shocks may still break historical symbolic patterns.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Future Directions.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Scalability}: Implement parallel MCTS and GPU-accelerated expression evaluation.
    \item \textbf{Portfolio Extension}: Discover expressions capturing cross-asset relationships for portfolio optimization.
    \item \textbf{Online Learning}: Develop continuous adaptation mechanisms that update expressions without full retraining.
    \item \textbf{Causal Discovery}: Incorporate causal inference to distinguish predictive relationships from spurious correlations.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Broader Impact.} Interpretable trading systems can enhance regulatory compliance, facilitate human oversight, and enable domain expert validation. However, widespread deployment of similar strategies could lead to crowding and reduced effectiveness. Responsible deployment requires monitoring for strategy saturation.
